Rocket Launch Alert System

You are part of the Launch Safety AI Division at the Aerospace Lab. Before every rocket launch, three sensor readings are collected. Your task is to predict whether a launch is likely to be safe (1) or unsafe (0) using a logistic regression model.

The model computes:

\( z = w_1 x_1 + w_2 x_2 + w_3 x_3 + b \)

\(\hat{p} = \sigma(z) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-z}} \)

\(\hat{y} = f(\hat{p}) \)

Based on the output probability \(\hat{p}\), the launch is classified as:

  • \( \hat{p} \geq 0.5 \) → \( \hat{y} = 1 \, \) → Safe Launch
  • \( \hat{p} < 0.5 \) → \( \hat{y} = 0 \, \) → Unsafe Launch

For a new launch test, the onboard computer reports:

  • Sensor input: \( \mathbf{x} = [{{params.x1}}, {{params.x2}}, {{params.x3}}] \)
  • Weights: \( \mathbf{w} = [{{params.w1}}, {{params.w2}}, {{params.w3}}] \)
  • Bias: \( b = {{params.b}} \)

What will your model predict? Enter 1 for a safe launch, or 0 for an unsafe launch.
