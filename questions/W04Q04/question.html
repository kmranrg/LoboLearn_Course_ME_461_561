Understanding the Perceptron Algorithm

The Perceptron is one of the earliest and simplest algorithms for binary classification. It makes predictions using the rule:

\( \hat{y} = \text{sign}(\mathbf{w}^\top \mathbf{x} + b) \)

where:

  • \( \mathbf{x} \) is the input vector,
  • \( \mathbf{w} \) is the weight vector,
  • \( b \) is the bias.

During training, the Perceptron updates its weights when it makes a mistake on a training example.
